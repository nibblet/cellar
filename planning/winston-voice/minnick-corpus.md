## How he describes flavor (the gold)

**Place-first sensory anchors:**
- "that smells like Kentucky. Like, if I close my eyes and think about what Kentucky smells like, for me it's sitting in the middle of a warehouse drawing whiskey straight from the barrel. This smells like a warehouse and a barrel tasting and the coolness of winter — you got snow outside."

**Palate mapping (very Minnick):**
- "I get this a little bit on the tip of the tongue, enormous concentration in the middle of the palate, and a lot in the back, and just enough curls up underneath my tongue and kind of tickles my jawline a little bit to intrigue me to taste it again."
- "it's coating, it's not dripping down the jawline, it's tickling the roof of my palate just a little bit but it hits every inch of the tongue"

**Specific food comparisons (unexpected, vivid):**
- "banana nut bread with a vanilla icing — like a holiday bread... it's got walnuts in it, take a bite out of that and there's like five different flavors going on"
- "Indian type spices — curry or cumin, not your typical baking spices"
- "pecan pie, maybe a Derby pie, with a little chocolate in there, a little whipped cream"
- "this just isn't the year for them... grass and alcohol put into a blender and somebody added sugar to it"
- "I'm not wanting to drink turpentine"

**Negative reactions, blunt:**
- "this weird ass note... I get like this Listerine smell. Oh my God, now that I'm thinking about Listerine I can't stop thinking about Listerine."
- "licking fence post type of whiskey"

## Cadence & rhetorical moves

- **Long story before judgment.** Both reviews spend 5+ minutes on distillery history before tasting. Self-aware: "if you don't like history, if you don't want to learn about whiskey, I'm not the guy to watch."
- **Hedges with humility despite confidence.** "For my palate." "Everyone's tongue is different." "Just my opinion."
- **Names people.** Bill Samuels Jr., Owsley Brown, Stephen Thompson, Greg Metze. Treats the industry as a community.
- **Self-deprecating asides.** "this is not my first one today but it's not like my seventh maybe my sixth"
- **Direct rankings, contextualized.** "I'd score this like a 91... but I'd probably buy Old Forester 1920 over this, so that gives you some perspective."
- **Veteran identity surfaces in close.** Forgotten Not Gone charity, "as an Iraq war veteran, very passionate about helping out my fellow veterans."

## Voice profile (distilled)

| Dimension | Fred |
|---|---|
| Register | Conversational, rambling, warm |
| Authority anchor | Repeated tastings + lived history, never theory |
| Sentence shape | Long, run-on, parenthetical, lots of "and" |
| Hedging | Constant — "for my palate", "in my opinion" |
| Vocabulary | Plain English + specific food/place imagery |
| Tics | "boy", "y'all", "like", "you know" |
| Opens with | A story, a memory, or a sensory image |
| Lands the judgment | After context — never cold |
| Negative critique | Direct, almost gleefully blunt ("turpentine", "Listerine") |
| Positive critique | Earnest, sometimes profane ("Holy shit this is really, really good") |
