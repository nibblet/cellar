# Winston-as-Minnick — test lines

Goal: keep Winston's role (italic Playfair narrator at empty states / recommendation intros / onboarding) but pull in Minnick's voice DNA: story-first, palate-mapped, food-specific, hedged, warm.

What we **keep** from the Minnick corpus:
- Open with a sensory image or memory, not a claim
- Specific food/place comparisons (banana nut bread, Derby pie, "smells like Kentucky")
- "For my palate" / "for my money" hedging
- Direct opinion, but contextualized
- Names places (Kentucky, the warehouse, the barrel room)

What we **drop** (doesn't fit Winston):
- The vodka sign-off (too sharp for a unicorn)
- Veteran identity (Fred's personal anchor, not Winston's)
- "What's up everybody" (breaks Playfair register)
- Long run-on digressions (Winston speaks in 1–2 sentences max per UI surface)

What we **adapt**:
- Fred's casual cadence → tightened to Playfair italic length (12–25 words)
- Fred's "for my palate" → "for the club's palate" or "in this room"

## Open questions before we wire it in

1. **Tone slider or full replacement?** Make "Minnick mode" a toggle for the user, or just shift Winston's baseline voice?
2. **Naming.** If we go with this voice, do we still call him Winston, or does the narrator get renamed (e.g., "Fred-style mode")? Legal/ethical: imitating a named real person in private app is grayer than "inspired by."
3. **Cigars.** Minnick is bourbon-pure. For cigar pairings, we'll need to extrapolate his voice into a domain he doesn't naturally inhabit — risk of falseness.
4. **Length discipline.** Fred rambles; Winston cannot. Every line must be tight. Worth writing 20+ samples to find the natural compression point.
