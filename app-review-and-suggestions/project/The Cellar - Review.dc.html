The Cellar

A private humidor & bourbon app with a mature engine and a real voice. It's also one migration from going live, still haunted by its cigar-club past, and sitting on infrastructure you haven't cashed in. Here's the honest read — and eight moves to supercharge it.

The hard parts are done, and done well. Photo capture with GPT vision ID, an Apify-fed enrichment pipeline, a rules-based pairing engine over trait vectors, a per-palate taste model, and Winston as an actual brand voice. The design system is disciplined — one primary action per screen, three accents with exactly one job each, etched dividers, silent flavor-wheel math. Most apps at this stage are held together with tape; this one has 400+ passing library tests. The gap isn't capability. It's the last mile: getting it live, finishing the solo identity, and turning built-but-buried infrastructure into things you actually touch.

Do this first · P0

Ship it

The single highest-leverage action isn't a feature — it's a supabase db push. The fork now runs against an isolated cellar schema, but the app can't serve a single row until you (1) apply the clone migration and (2) add cellar to Supabase's Exposed Schemas. Everything below is moot until someone can sign in. Do it today.

Also broken at the door: the PWA manifest points at four icon files that don't exist (FIX-031), so "Add to Home Screen" — the entire point of a PWA — installs a broken icon. And there's no error.tsx or not-found.tsx, so any stumble drops the user onto raw Next.js scaffolding. Three small fixes stand between "deployed" and "feels real."

Then · P1

Finish the fork — exorcise the club

Right now it reads a little like "the club app with the club parts deleted" rather than an app designed for one person. The tells:

  • Winston speaks where he's forbidden. <Voice/> appears on the capture, recommend, and pairing-capture forms (FIX-028/033/041) — your own design system says he never shows on capture. Six sites, one sweep.
  • Ghost labels. "Club staple" still renders on product detail, and the "Your cellar" header prints your own name back at you via formatMemberName — vestigial for an audience of one.
  • Moss with no job. The accent was reassigned to "on your shelf," but dormant group-validation stamps still linger on a few surfaces. Retire them, or the color lies.
  • Name it. The masthead still says NCCC. "The Cellar" deserves its own wordmark before launch — the cheapest identity win you have.
And · P1

Fix what's quietly broken

A few bugs are silently taxing the intelligence you built:

  • Critical — cigars never enrich. A stray return false in needs-enrichment.ts (FIX-038) means no cigar ever triggers Apify + wheel-vector enrichment. Half your catalog runs on vision-only specs, which quietly starves the pairing engine of exactly the signal it needs. One-line delete, outsized payoff.
  • Open enrichment endpoint. api/enrich-draft has no ownership check (FIX-040) — any authed user can spend your OpenAI/Apify budget on any draft. Solo today, but a real hole.
  • Small correctness leaks. Orphaned storage files on failed inserts (several sites) and UTC date math that flips "Tonight's Pick" at 8pm EDT (FIX-024/025). Individually minor; collectively the gap between "polished" and "almost."
Supercharge — the six bets

With the above cleared, here's where the next few evenings pay off — ranked by leverage over effort. Each builds on something already in the codebase.

01

Ask Winston — a conversational front door

Leverage · Very high Effort · Medium

You already shipped an MCP server exposing nine tools — search, pairings, similar, tonight's pick, try-next, your cellar — to external AI clients. Your own app doesn't use them. Wire a single natural-language bar ("What pairs with a maduro tonight?" / "What should I buy next?") that routes to those exact tools and answers in Winston's voice. The hard part already exists. This is the moment the app stops being a database with screens and becomes a butler.

02

Make stocking frictionless

Leverage · High Effort · Medium

Capture is the moat, and a cold cellar is a dead taste engine. Two moves: a day-one "mark what you own" sweep over the catalog (the bulk-backfill you explicitly deferred), so the palate model has signal on night one; and batch capture — photograph a shelf, confirm a stack — so stocking 30 bottles isn't 30 round-trips. Fast in, smart out.

03

Add pour depth

Leverage · High Effort · Medium

The have / want / tried / loved model is elegant but binary — a bottle is on the shelf or it isn't. The natural next dimension (and the one you deferred as Phase 5.5) is how much is left: full → heel → empty, "finished on this date," and a quiet "running low" nudge. This is what turns a wishlist into a living cellar and unlocks restock reminders — without adding a single number to the UI.

04

A palate portrait on the home screen

Leverage · Medium Effort · Low–Med

Your flavor wheel is deliberately silent — good discipline. But the aggregate of your own tastings is a beautiful, honest, non-numeric signature waiting to be drawn: a tag-cloud "portrait" of your palate ("you lean sweet, oak, full-proof") rendered as the hero of the Cellar home, not a buried insight card. It respects the no-sliders rule and finally gives the app a face.

05

Context-aware Tonight's Pick

Leverage · Medium Effort · Low

The pick is already deterministic-daily and even says "for a Tuesday on the porch." Let it actually know the porch: time of day, season, and weather nudging the choice (a cold night leans richer). A small model change with disproportionate delight — it makes Winston feel present rather than scripted.

06

Close the loop — share & reach

Leverage · Medium Effort · Low

Two cheap wins already scoped in your backlog: a native navigator.share() sheet on product pages (IDEA-018) so a great pour leaves the app as a clean card, and bourbon-specific explore links (IDEA-017). Small, but they're the difference between a private log and something that occasionally travels.

Signature polish
  • Elevate the home. "Your cellar" is currently a stack of dividers over a plain list. Give it one hero moment — Tonight's Pick as a real card with both labels, then the shelf at a glance — so opening the app feels like walking into the room, not reading a table.
  • Lean into the wink. The etched dividers and Winston's dry serif are your best assets. Use them deliberately at section breaks and empty states — less as ambient decoration everywhere.
  • Motion like a closing door. Your own doc asks for restraint — 400ms reveals, no bounce. The photo-to-ID reveal is the signature interaction; make it the one animation you truly polish.

"The bottles are catalogued, the palate is known, the pairings are sound. All that remains, sir, is to open the door — and perhaps stop mentioning the club."
